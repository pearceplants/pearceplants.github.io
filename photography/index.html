Photography

Over the past few years, I’ve developed an obsession with using macro photography to document the amazing diversity of flower shape, color, and function on our planet. My setup involves placing plants in a light box with a black felt background, and then using a technique called ‘focus stacking’ to combine multiple close-up pictures into a single ‘stacked’ image with extremely high resolution and depth of field.

I enjoy taking plant samples and dissecting them to get photographs of every part of their anatomy. I believe that these comprehensive macro photo sets have many practical uses, which I will list below.

Education

Teaching floral anatomy can be difficult due to the dense quantity of unfamiliar terms and the morphological complexity of many flower structures. When describing a new species, it is helpful to have pictures or illustrations to depict this new taxon. My photography could showcase every minute detail on each part of a plant species, as in the above and below collages, serving as a visual reference to assist the verbal description.

Identification

Identifying plants to species often requires viewing minute morphological details, with subtle differences between species that are hard to interpret via text-based keys alone. Macro photography provides a visual example to help unambiguously clear up these confusions.

For instance, one key trait separating Stephanomeria pauciflora (left) from most other Stephanomeria (right, in this case S. occultata) is that S. pauciflora has “…pappus tan, plumose on the distal 80%”, whereas most other species have “…pappus white, wholly plumose”.

This description can be difficult to parse, and if you are trying to key out a single specimen, it may be tough to determine which keybreak is more appropriate without having the other option with which to compare. However, viewing the differences side by side in the below picture, the distinction is clear.

Thus, providing these comparative macro photographs alongside keys gives a convenient visual resource to compare the options, making keying faster, more accurate, and more intuitive.
